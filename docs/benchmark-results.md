## Interpretation

Phase 5 replaced O(n) `findIndex` walks with an `Identifier → index` map maintained on insert. Lookups used by `insert` (resolve `leftOrigin`) and `delete` (resolve `targetId`) are now O(1).

## Next bottleneck at larger scale

Lookup is no longer the hot path. Remaining O(n) costs per insert:

1. **`Array.splice`** to place the new node in the sequence array
2. **Index-map shift** — every map entry at or after the splice point is incremented
3. **Walk-right** among concurrent siblings (usually short; worst-case O(n))

At hundreds of thousands of characters, a tree-backed sequence (e.g. a balanced binary tree or rope keyed by Identifier order) would make both placement and index maintenance logarithmic. Tombstone GC would also matter once deleted nodes dominate memory.
